# Wu Pao-chun: From Rural Pingtung to World Bread Champion

## Starting Point: A Junior High Graduate's Apprenticeship

Wu Pao-chun was born into a farming family in Neipu Township, Pingtung County, in humble circumstances. After graduating from junior high school, he went north to become a bread apprentice. In that era, baking masters were blue-collar among blue-collar workers, and no one believed making bread could lead to greatness.

However, Wu Pao-chun possessed an almost obsessive passion for bread. Starting from the most basic dough kneading, he spent over a decade refining his technique, teaching himself French to read French baking books and traveling to Japan to learn from world-class bread masters. He elevated bread-making from a "means of livelihood" to a "craftsman's cultivation."

## World Champion

In 2008, Wu Pao-chun and his team won the runner-up position at the Coupe du Monde de la Boulangerie (World Cup of Baking) in Paris, France. Two years later in 2010, he claimed the championship at the Mondial du Pain (World Bread Masters Competition) with his "Lychee Rose Bread."

This bread featured Taiwan's local dried lychee and organic rose petals as the main ingredients, combining them with French baking techniques to create an unprecedented combination of taste and aroma. Judges described it as "a perfect dialogue between Eastern flavors and Western techniques."

Wu Pao-chun became the first Taiwanese to win an individual championship at an international bread competition, triggering an unprecedented "bread fever" across Taiwan.

## Returning to Taiwan

After winning the championship, Wu Pao-chun chose not to remain in France or move to other culinary capitals, but returned to Taiwan to open a bakery. His first shop opened in Kaohsiung, where queuing crowds became part of the city's landscape.

Wu Pao-chun's business philosophy was: using Taiwan's local ingredients to make world-class bread. Red quinoa, dried longan, mango, pineapple—these Taiwanese agricultural products became the soul of bread in his hands.

## Education and Legacy

Wu Pao-chun has invested in baking education, establishing a baking academy to train the next generation of masters. He continues to return to Pingtung, supporting local agriculture and education.

His story touches people's hearts not only because of his championship title, but because of his background: a junior high graduate from the countryside with no resources or connections, who reached the top of the world through obsession with one thing. In Taiwan, the name "Wu Pao-chun" has become almost synonymous with "craftsman spirit."
