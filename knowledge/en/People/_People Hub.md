On February 29, 1993, in Los Angeles, when Ang Lee accepted the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film, his first words were "Thank you, I am proud to be Taiwanese." At that moment, the world's attention turned to this director from Taiwan, and to the small island behind him. From then on, Taiwanese people began hearing their name on the international stage, seeing their own reflections, feeling applause from the world.

📖 Further Reading: [[Ang Lee]]

Taiwan — this island of only 36,000 square kilometers with a population of just 23 million — has nonetheless nurtured an astonishing density of talent. Here, on average, every one million people might produce a Nobel laureate, an Oscar winner, a world champion, or an internationally recognized entrepreneur. This is not coincidence but a reflection of this land's unique and irreplaceable appeal.

From Morris Chang building the TSMC empire that would become the linchpin of global digital civilization, to Lin Hwai-min making Cloud Gate Dance Theatre "a necessary landscape of the world" on the international stage; from Tsai Ing-wen breaking the glass ceiling for women leaders throughout the Chinese-speaking world, to Tai Tzu-ying displaying Taiwan athletes' resilient and elegant will on the badminton court — these Taiwan luminaries are not only embodiments of personal achievement, but the finest ambassadors of the Taiwan spirit: tenacious in adversity, innovative in opportunity, and vocal in the world.

## Cultural Ambassadors: Souls Who Conquered the World Through Creation

**Ang Lee** — The pioneer who opened Taiwan cinema to the world. From the "Father Trilogy" — "Pushing Hands," "The Wedding Banquet," "Eat Drink Man Woman" — Lee moved Western audiences with the delicate emotional textures of Eastern family life. "Crouching Tiger Hidden Dragon" revealed to the world the poetic beauty latent in Chinese martial arts culture. "Brokeback Mountain" challenged entrenched social values with quiet grace. "Life of Pi" carried deep philosophical thinking about survival, faith, and the nature of reality. Two Academy Awards for Best Director, three Golden Bears, two Golden Lions — a record that speaks for itself.

**Lin Hwai-min** founded Cloud Gate Dance Theatre in 1973 and spent 46 years making Taiwan dance "a necessary landscape of the world." From "Legacy" to "Legend of the White Snake," from "Nine Songs" to "Smoke," Cloud Gate fuses Tai Chi movement, calligraphy, and Beijing opera gestures into modern dance, creating a unique vocabulary of Eastern contemporary movement that belongs to no tradition yet honors all of them. The New York Times called it "Asia's most important modern dance company" after its performances at Lincoln Center.

**Hou Hsiao-hsien** — the defining leader of Taiwan New Wave Cinema. "A City of Sadness" won the Golden Lion at the Venice Film Festival — the first Taiwan film to claim the top honor at any of the world's major international film festivals. The "Hou-style aesthetics" of long takes and static wide shots became a recognizable poetic visual language, depicting Taiwan's historical upheavals and the quiet landscape of human endurance with extraordinary sensitivity.

## Technology Pioneers: Engineers Who Created the Silicon Island Miracle

**Morris Chang** — dubbed "Father of Semiconductors" for good reason. He founded TSMC single-handedly, changing Taiwan's destiny and redefining the global semiconductor industry's division of labor. In 1987, when Chang proposed the concept of the "pure-play foundry," many industry veterans thought it impossible. Yet this "impossible" model made TSMC the world's largest foundry and gave Taiwan the strategic "silicon shield" that has defined 21st-century geopolitics.

Today TSMC's market capitalization exceeds $500 billion — equivalent to the entirety of Taiwan's annual GDP. When Apple needs the world's most advanced processors, when NVIDIA launches its next generation of AI chips, when governments around the world convene emergency summits over semiconductor supply security — they all think of TSMC, and of the business civilization that Morris Chang created.

**Stan Shih** — pioneer of Taiwan's brand internationalization. He founded Acer Computer and built it into a globally recognized PC brand, proving that a Taiwan company could compete on international shelves. More importantly, Shih's "Smile Curve" theory — which argues that R&D design and brand marketing hold the highest value in any supply chain while manufacturing assembly holds the least — profoundly shaped Taiwan manufacturing's long-term transformation thinking.

## Political Leaders: Those Who Led Democratic Taiwan

**Tsai Ing-wen** — Taiwan's first female president, and the first democratically elected female national leader in the Chinese-speaking world. From international trade law scholar to the highest office in the land, Tsai's political journey showcases the openness and meritocracy of Taiwan's democratic system. She proposed the "Taiwan Model" — emphasizing Taiwan's achievements in democracy, technology, and public health — helping the international community rediscover Taiwan's genuine value as a partner. During COVID-19, her government's prevention achievements were held up internationally as among the world's most effective.

**Lee Teng-hui** — dubbed "Mr. Democracy" by those who watched him work. His democratic reforms in the late 1980s peacefully transformed Taiwan from an authoritarian society to a functioning democracy, without the violence that accompanied similar transitions elsewhere. The "Quiet Revolution's" success made Taiwan one of the most celebrated cases in the Third Wave of Democracy. Lee was also an intellectual of uncommon range; his advocacy for Taiwan consciousness profoundly shaped the island's political and cultural self-understanding.

## Academic Giants: The Wise Ones Standing at Knowledge's Peak

**Yuan T. Lee** won the 1986 Nobel Prize in Chemistry — the first Nobel laureate born in Taiwan. His foundational research in chemical reaction dynamics provided tools that transformed scientists' ability to understand reaction mechanisms at the microscopic level. Under his leadership, Academia Sinica became one of Asia's most respected academic research institutions.

**Ta-You Wu** — dubbed "Father of Chinese Physics" for his internationally recognized contributions to theoretical physics. More importantly, Wu nurtured and inspired generations of physics talent, including Nobel laureates Yang Chen-Ning and Tsung-Dao Lee, whose 1957 Nobel Prize in Physics helped establish Taiwan's scientific reputation on the world stage.

## Sports Heroes: Taiwan's Stars Shining on the Competitive Stage

**Tai Tzu-ying** — a legendary figure in Taiwan badminton and a generational athlete by any standard. She held the world number-one ranking for 214 consecutive weeks, a historic record. At the 2021 Tokyo Olympics, she won the badminton women's singles silver medal in one of the tournament's most talked-about finals. It is not only her technical brilliance but her composure under pressure and fierce resilience in adversity that have made her "Little Tai spirit" an inspiration to countless young Taiwanese.

**Chien-Ming Wang** — once the ace pitcher for the New York Yankees and one of the most dominant right-handed pitchers in the American League. His MLB success sent all Taiwan into "Wang fever." Though injury later affected his career, his standing in Taiwan baseball history remains unshakeable.

**Wei-Yin Chen** — a consistent and reliable representative of Taiwan's US-based baseball players. From Japanese professional baseball to MLB, Chen demonstrated the quiet strength and professionalism that Taiwan's baseball culture produces.

## New Generation Taiwan Luminaries

**Audrey Tang** — the world's first transgender government minister, whose innovations in digital governance have attracted international recognition and study. The vTaiwan platform, the JOIN platform, and a series of other digital democracy experiments have placed Taiwan at the world's forefront in civic technology and participatory governance. Tang's success is not only a personal achievement but a demonstration of Taiwan society's remarkable inclusiveness and its courage to imagine governance differently.

**Wu Che-yu** — operating at the intersection of art and technology, this new media artist creates "living" algorithmic life forms through code, with works exhibited at the Venice Biennale, Art Basel Miami, and Paris CENTQUATRE-PARIS. He has also brought over 20,000 students into the world of interactive art programming through online courses. In 2026, he launched the Taiwan.md open knowledge base project — using an AI-friendly Markdown format, written and reviewed by Taiwanese people themselves, covering everything about Taiwan, building Taiwan's own digital anthropology museum. In the AI era, owning your knowledge sovereignty is not optional; it is necessary.

**Sports New Stars**: The 2021 Tokyo Olympics saw Taiwan's delegation write the best-ever performance in its Olympic history. Kuo Hsing-chun won gold in women's weightlifting 59kg while setting three Olympic records — cementing her "Weightlifting Goddess" status. Wang Chi-lin and Lee Yang (the "Lin Yang Pei" pair) won Taiwan's first ever Olympic gold medal in badminton — their celebratory pose became a national collective memory. Yang Yung-wei won judo silver, writing a new page in Taiwan judo Olympic history. Lee Chih-kai transformed from "Tumbling Boy" to "Pommel Horse Prince," winning silver with a signature Thomas salto that had crowds on their feet.

**Cultural Creation**: In imagery and design, Chi Po-lin awakened national environmental consciousness with "Beyond Beauty — Taiwan from Above," his transformative aerial documentary. Aaron Nieh defined contemporary Taiwan design aesthetics with a minimalist yet emotionally powerful visual language. Crowd Lu, with a grass-roots authenticity that spans music and film and television, is one of Taiwan's rare "Triple Crown" artists — recognized by the Golden Melody, Golden Bell, and Golden Horse Awards alike.

**Culinary Arts**: André Chiang, one of Asia's most influential chefs, returned to Taiwan after voluntarily returning his Michelin stars, redefining the dialogue between "Taiwanese flavors" and French cuisine through his RAW restaurant, and inspiring a new generation of culinary craftspeople to look inward for their creative source.

**Technology Revolution**: Jensen Huang founded NVIDIA and led the global AI computing revolution that is reshaping every industry on Earth. As the de facto "Father of AI hardware," he not only demonstrated leadership at the apex of technology but has actively given back to his homeland — deepening strategic connections with Taiwan's semiconductor industry, particularly TSMC, making Taiwan the indispensable core of the global AI map.

Behind the success of these Taiwanese people reflects the unique values of this land: an open social environment, a quality education system, a democratic political system, a diverse cultural atmosphere. Their stories tell the world — Taiwan may be small, but it can nurture great minds that change the world; Taiwan people may be few, but they can display outstanding creativity in every field.
