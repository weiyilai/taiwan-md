# Taiwan's Incense-Making Culture and Heritage

## 30-Second Overview

In Taiwan's traditional religious culture, incense serves not only as a medium connecting people with deities but also carries forward a handicraft art that has continued for hundreds of years. From the "incense stick hometown" of Chiayi's Yunxiao Community to Lukang's century-old incense shops and Xingang's incense arts cultural park, Taiwan's incense-making industry witnesses the deep foundation of the island's folk beliefs and craftspeople's dedicated preservation of traditional techniques.

**Key Features:** Handicraft skills, religious culture, local industry, craftsman spirit, incense stick flowers

## Why It Matters

Incense-making culture embodies the close connection between Taiwan's folk beliefs and daily life. In a highly modernized society, incense masters who insist on traditional methods not only preserve ancient craft techniques but also maintain the cultural spirit that "when incense is proper, the heart of worship will be proper." For understanding Taiwan's religious life, handicraft inheritance, and the development trajectory of local industries, incense-making culture provides an excellent observation window.

Through the incense industry, we see how Taiwan maintains cultural roots in the wave of globalization, how it treasures handmade warmth in the age of mechanization, and how local communities form unique cultural identities around traditional crafts.

## Incense Stick Hometown: Chiayi Yunxiao Community

### The Century-Old Story of Incense Stick Flowers

Located near the Dizang Temple in Chiayi City, Yunxiao Community developed an incense stick splitting industry spanning over a century due to its close connection with this temple rich in incense offerings. In those days of hardship, women in Yunxiao Community all mastered skilled incense stick splitting techniques to help support their families, with almost every household engaged in incense stick production.

Yunxiao Community was once the largest incense stick production area in southern Taiwan, earning the reputation as the "incense stick hometown." Locals spread a saying of praise: "Yunxiao houses bloom flowers all year round—the blooming incense stick flowers," vividly describing the bustling scene when every household was making incense sticks. Those exquisite bamboo fibers were tied into beautiful mushroom shapes, like blooming flowers, becoming Yunxiao Community's most unique local scenery.

### Craftspeople's Skills and Persistence

**Master incense stick artist Qiu Jin-yun's** story encapsulates the entire industry's historical trajectory. She began learning incense stick splitting from her mother in elementary school and continued until handmade incense sticks were replaced by machine production. In her skilled hands, splitting bamboo with a wood knife was as easy as waving her hand, and she understood bamboo characteristics thoroughly. In just a minute or two, thin bamboo strips could become finger-soft bamboo rope, with numerous incense sticks tied into beautiful mushroom-shaped incense stick flowers.

**Master incense maker Cai Zeng-cheng** demonstrated another aspect of incense-making processes. In front of a nearly 2-meter round basin, he would soak prepared incense sticks in water, then perform continuous actions of spreading, rolling, shaking, trembling, and rubbing, allowing incense powder based on traditional Chinese medicine and sandalwood to adhere evenly to each incense stick. The same process needed to be repeated 4 to 6 times to create handmade refined clear incense of different specifications: chi 3, chi 6, chi 7, chi 8.

Master Cai emphasized that in the past, one master had to make 150 jin (approximately 90 kg) of incense per day. Handmade incense making is an industry of conscience and dedication. Each stick of incense must have a beautiful pointed tip and graceful ending, uniform from top to bottom without being thick or thin, and the incense stick must be perfectly straight. He often said: "Every stick of incense must be beautiful and burn well, cannot be crooked or have broken sticks. When incense is proper, the heart of worship will be proper; when incense burns beautifully, worshippers' hearts will naturally be at peace."

## Taiwan-Wide Incense Industry Map

### Lukang: Century-Old Incense Shop Heritage

The incense industry in Lukang, Changhua, has an equally long history, with **Shi Jin-yu Mu Xiang Zhai** having been passed down for 260 years through eight generations. Lukang's incense industry is renowned for its exquisite handicraft skills, particularly in the selection of incense materials. Old agarwood is treasure excavated from underground through geological movements, having absorbed the essence of sun and moon for hundreds or even thousands of years, making it more precious than sandalwood harvested after only 60 to 100 years, and is considered the finest among incenses.

Lukang's incense industry faces competition from low-priced incense products from mainland China, but by insisting on quality and natural raw materials, it has successfully maintained the middle and upper-tier customer market. Modern people's demand for incense has expanded from purely religious purposes to life stress relief and environmental purification, creating new market spaces for traditional incense industries.

### Xingang: New Model of Cultural Tourism

Xingang in Chiayi established Taiwan's first cultural park themed around "incense"—**Xingang Incense Arts Cultural Park**—elevating the traditional incense industry to the cultural arts level. This park not only preserves incense-making crafts but also allows visitors to deeply understand incense's important position in Taiwanese culture through exhibitions and experiential activities.

The Xingang model demonstrates the possibilities for traditional industry transformation: from pure manufacturing to cultural tourism, maintaining craft spirit while injecting new vitality into the industry.

### Other Regional Incense Centers

Tainan, Changhua's Hemei, Yilan's Toucheng, and other places also have important incense manufacturers, forming an island-wide incense industry network. These incense producers have each developed different characteristics and specializations—some specialize in religious incense, others develop lifestyle aromatherapy markets, collectively maintaining the diversity of Taiwan's incense culture.

## The Essence of Incense-Making Crafts

### Selection and Processing of Raw Materials

Traditional incense making primarily uses natural raw materials, including sandalwood, agarwood, dragon's blood, and various aromatic woods, combined with traditional Chinese medicinal materials like cloves, cinnamon, and nardostachys. Incense sticks mainly use bamboo, requiring the selection of dense, well-elastic bamboo materials, processed through splitting, drying, and other steps.

### Complete Incense-Making Process

1. **Incense stick production**: Split bamboo into fine fibers, tie into bundles
2. **Incense powder blending**: Mix various aromatics according to traditional formulas
3. **Powder coating**: Dip incense sticks in water then roll in incense powder
4. **Repeated coating**: Repeat 4 to 6 times to ensure even powder adhesion
5. **Drying and setting**: Natural air drying or kiln drying
6. **Quality inspection and packaging**: Select high-quality incense for packaging

### Quality Assessment Standards

Characteristics of high-quality handmade incense include: straight incense sticks without bending, evenly distributed incense powder, ash that doesn't easily fall during burning, and pure fragrance without harsh smells. Traditional masters emphasize that truly good incense should be "proper incense, proper heart," bringing peaceful and serene feelings when burned.

## Industry Challenges and Transformation

### Mechanization and Import Competition

With the development of mechanical incense production technology and low-priced incense products from mainland China entering the market, Taiwan's traditional handmade incense making faces severe challenges. Many old masters are aging, and fewer young people are willing to enter this difficult trade, creating a crisis in skill transmission.

### Transformation and Innovation

Facing challenges, Taiwan's incense industry actively seeks transformation:

1. **Quality differentiation**: Insisting on natural raw materials, winning through quality
2. **Market expansion**: Expanding from religious markets to lifestyle aromatherapy markets
3. **Cultural tourism**: Combining tourism experiences to promote incense culture
4. **Brand management**: Building brand images, enhancing product added value

### Efforts in Skill Transmission

To prevent incense culture from being lost, many second-generation successors abandon other career opportunities, choosing to stay home to inherit incense-making businesses. While maintaining traditional techniques, they also attempt to integrate modern management concepts, injecting new vitality into the ancient incense industry.

## Spiritual Connotations of Incense Culture

### Connection Between Religion and Life

In Taiwan's religious life, incense plays an important role in communicating between people and deities. Lighting incense for worship is not only a religious ritual but also embodies people's reverence for deities, remembrance of ancestors, and prayers for a beautiful life. Incense masters deeply understand this responsibility, therefore maintaining devout hearts when making each stick of incense.

### Embodiment of Craftsman Spirit

Taiwan's incense culture embodies profound craftsman spirit: persistence in quality, adherence to tradition, and refinement of techniques. These incense masters often work for decades, honing skills in repetitive processes and deepening understanding of incense through accumulated years. They are not just making products but transmitting a way of life and cultural values.

### Extended Modern Significance

In modern life, incense's meaning has transcended purely religious uses. Lighting good incense can be a moment of tranquility in busy life, atmosphere creation for home spaces, or emotional connection with traditional culture. This transformation from practical function to spiritual comfort demonstrates traditional crafts' adaptability and vitality in modern society.

## Extended Reflections

The preservation and development of Taiwan's incense culture reflects a larger issue: how do we handle relationships with traditional culture in the process of rapid modernization? The case of the incense industry tells us that traditional crafts' survival cannot rely solely on sentiment and protection but needs to find new positioning and value in changing times.

From Yunxiao Community's incense stick flowers to Xingang's cultural park, from Lukang's century-old incense shops to modern brand management, Taiwan's incense culture is undergoing profound transformation. This transformation process is both a challenge and an opportunity—how to embrace innovation while maintaining craft spirit, how to maintain cultural characteristics in globalization waves, how to make traditional techniques bloom again in modern society are all issues worth continued attention.

For those wanting to understand Taiwan's cultural depth, incense culture provides an excellent entry point: it is both a carrier of religious culture and a crystallization of handicraft art; it has historical depth and modern transformation. Behind every wisp of rising incense smoke lies craftspeople's wisdom, the power of faith, and the profound significance of cultural transmission.
